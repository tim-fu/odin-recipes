Kevin's Famous Chili

It's probably the thing Kevin does best.

Description

A recipe passed down from Malones for generations.

This big bowl of happiness is perfect for the entire office — best served family style to boost coworkers' morale.

Ingredients

  • Large, heavy steel pot
  • 4 dried ancho chiles
  • 2 tbs vegetable or canola oil
  • 3 lbs ground beef
  • 2 medium yellow onions, finely chopped
  • 6 cloves garlic
  • 1 large jalapeño
  • 1 tbs dried oregano
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
  • 2 tbs tomato paste
  • 2 12 oz bottles of beer (lager or pale ale)
  • 3 cans pinto beans, drained and rinsed
  • 3 cups beef stock
  • 2 1/2 cups chopped ripe tomatoes
  • 2 tbs kosher salt
  • Chopped scallions, shredded Jack cheese, sour cream for topping
  • File folders to mop up the mess

Steps

  1. Tear ancho chiles into pieces, discarding seeds and stems. In a large heavy pot, toast chiles over medium-high, stirring occasionally until very fragrant, 3 to 4 min. Process chiles in a food processor or spice mill until finely grounded.
  2. Add oil to pot and heat over medium-high. Add ground beef and cook, stirring occasionally to break beef into small pieces, until well browned (about 6 min). Using a slotted spoon, transfer beef to a plate.
  3. Add onion to pot and cook briefly over medium-high until barely softened, about 2 min. The secret is to undercook the onions.
  4. Using a garlic press, press garlic directly into the pot. Then stir in jalapeños, oregano, cumin, cayenne pepper and tomato paste. Stir and cook until fragrant, about 2 min. Add beer and continue to cook, stirring and scraping the pan, about 7 min.
  5. Put beans in a large bowl and mash briefly with a potato masher until broken up but not fully mashed.
  6. Add mashed beans, stock, tomatoes, salt, and cooked beef to pot. Cover and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat to low to maintain simmer and cook 2 hours so everything gets to know each other in the pot. Remove from heat, uncover and let stand at least 1 hour (can also be refrigerated 8 hours or overnight).
  7. Reheat gently, taste and add more salt if necessary, and serve with your favorite toppings.
